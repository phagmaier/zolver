Performance Summary
  Your current approach is an "Exact DCFR" solver. It is very accurate but computationally expensive because it treats all 1326 hand combinations
  individually for every single calculation.

   * Current Speed: You are doing well with SIMD, but your biggest bottleneck is likely Sparsity. Currently, even if a player has only 1% of their hands
     left in a range, you are still calculating the other 99% as zeros.
   * Showdown: Your collision correction is O(H · C), which is the hottest part of your code. Moving to a Fast Walsh-Hadamard Transform (FWHT) or a more
     optimized table-based evaluator would be a major win.

  Key Optimization Roadmap
   1. Sparsity-Aware CFR (Highest Priority): Modify your walk to skip hands with zero reach. This is usually a 5x–10x speedup in post-flop scenarios.
   2. Regret Compression: Storing regrets as f32 is memory-intensive. Using 16-bit integers with a scaling factor can double your cache efficiency.
   3. Parallel Merging: As you expand your betting tree, the serial mergeDeltas step will become a significant bottleneck. This should be parallelized.

  Roadmap to a "Full-Fledged" Solver
  To compete with commercial solvers, you'll need to implement these structural changes:
   * Betting Tree Flexibility: Your current MAXNUMBETS = 2 and fixed BETSIZES are very restrictive. You need a system that allows arbitrary reraises and
     user-defined sizing.
   * Hand Abstraction (Bucketing): For pre-flop and flop solving, you cannot use all 1326 hands. You'll need to group them into ~500 clusters based on
     similarity.
   * Subgame Resolving: Your current subgame solving is a great start, but to be theoretically sound, you need CFR-D (Decomposition) to ensure subgame
     strategies remain part of the global equilibrium.
   * Rake: Real-world solvers require rake calculation, as it fundamentally changes the value of folding vs. calling.
